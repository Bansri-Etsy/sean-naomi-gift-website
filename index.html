How It All Began

Our Story

Every love story has a hero and a person who is just sort of... there. Naomi Priest is a woman of intelligence, grace, and impeccable judgement — except, apparently, in one notable area.

Sean Gleeson is a man who once described himself as "a catch." No one in the room agreed, but Naomi — blessed with either extraordinary patience or mild delusion — saw something the rest of us couldn't. We suspect it was pity.

What followed was a love story for the ages. Or at least a love story that has aged Sean considerably. Naomi, of course, looks better every year. Sean looks like he's been left in the sun too long.

He's not the man of my dreams, but he is the man who falls asleep during all of my dreams.
— Naomi, allegedly

The Journey

How We Got Here

The First Meeting

An Unremarkable Introduction

Naomi met Sean and thought, "He seems nice enough." Sean met Naomi and immediately began punching well above his weight. He has not stopped since. The referee has been asked to intervene on multiple occasions.

The First Date

Lowered Expectations

Sean planned what he called "a dead sophisticated evening." It was Nando's. To his credit, he did order the halloumi starter, which Naomi maintains is the only reason there was a second date. He also wore a shirt with a collar, which his mates later confirmed was borrowed.

Moving In Together

Naomi Discovers the Truth

Naomi learned that Sean owns four near-identical grey t-shirts and considers this "a capsule wardrobe." She also discovered his belief that dishes "soak themselves" and that leaving cupboard doors open is a personality trait, not a hazard. Love persevered.

The Proposal

He Actually Managed It

Against all reasonable expectation, Sean planned a genuinely lovely proposal. Naomi cried. Sean cried harder. The ring was beautiful — Naomi's best friend helped him choose it because, left to his own devices, Sean would have bought something from a market stall and called it "vintage."

A Note from the Bride

Why Him?

People ask me this constantly. Genuinely. At least once a week. My mother asks more often. The truth is, beneath the questionable fashion choices and the alarming number of football podcasts, there is a man with the biggest heart I've ever known. He makes me laugh every single day — mostly at him, occasionally with him. He is kind, gentle, and will absolutely cry reading this. I love him ridiculously.

He's also tall, which is useful for reaching things.

You Might Be Wondering

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I bring a plus one?

If your invitation says "and guest," absolutely. If it doesn't, we're afraid not — we love you, but we do not love you plus a person we've never met. Sean tried to invite his five-a-side team and was firmly overruled.

Are children welcome?

We adore your children. We really do. But this is an adults-only celebration. Think of it as a rare night off. You deserve it. Sean is basically a large child anyway, so the quota is filled.

What should I wear?

Formal attire, please. Suits, dresses, something you'd feel smashing in. The only person exempt from looking good is Sean, who will be doing his absolute best and we must all be supportive.

Is there parking?

Yes, The Grange has ample parking. However, given the open bar situation, we'd strongly recommend taxis. Sean's uncle has already volunteered to be designated driver, but we've seen him at weddings before. Don't trust it.

What about gifts?

Your presence is the greatest gift. Truly. But if you're feeling generous, we've set up a small honeymoon fund. Sean wanted to register at Argos. Naomi has handled it.

Will there be vegetarian options?

Absolutely. We're catering for all dietary requirements — just let us know on your RSVP. Sean didn't know what "dietary requirements" meant until 2024, but he's learning.
